CIC Leadership Playbook

Courageous  •  Innovative  •  Collaborative Rituals for Everyday Leadership

Purpose

This playbook helps managers shape the daily people experience—so work feels clear, meaningful, and energising. Grounded in the principles of The One Minute Manager, the idea is that good management doesn't require much time. Instead, small actions done consistently make the biggest difference.

Core Philosophy

People who feel good produce good work.

Wellbeing and performance are not separate.

The best investment you can make is in people.

Practice 1

One Minute Goals

Start the day ensuring your team knows what success looks like.

  • What are the top 1–3 priorities today?
  • What does "done" look like?
  • What might block progress?
  • How might we do things differently?
Example

"Today, the most important thing is X. If we achieve Y outcome, we're in a good place."

Practice 2

One Minute Praisings

Be specific on the collaborative behaviours. Tie to impact. Make it timely.

Examples
  • "I noticed [specific behaviour]. That's exactly the kind of thing that [impact]. Thank you."
  • "Thank you for [specific behaviour]. It matters because [reason]."
  • "I noticed [what you did] — and I want you to know that [why it counts]."
  • "Thank you for trying [a new approach/ something new]."
  • "Thank you for raising this [e.g. risk, assumption] early."
Practice 3

One Minute Redirects

Address issues early, factually, and privately. Focus on the issue, not the intent or character.

What happened
Why it matters
Pause for internalisation
What to do differently next time
Reaffirm the person
Example

"When X happened, the impact was Y. (pause for internalisation) Let's align on how we approach this moving forward."

Team Rituals

A dedicated space for professional development

  • Skills to build
  • Opportunities to stretch
  • Career direction

Dedicate time set aside to bond, have fun, and strengthen team relationships

  • Rotating share — Each member takes a turn sharing what's on their mind; an aha moment, life update, or work insight
  • Show & tell — Members take turns teaching the team one skill; a shortcut, recipe, cooking technique, or language phrase
  • After-work hangout — Casual get-togethers to wind down, bond, and build team memories

An intentional unstructured break to recharge after work

  • Movement break — Step away from your desk for a brisk walk, a quick Zumba session, or light stretches
  • Quiet creative time — Slow down and recharge by journaling or doodling; no agenda, just you and your thoughts
  • Intellect app — When you need a little extra support, tap into self-guided tools, coaching, or clinical care right from your phone
